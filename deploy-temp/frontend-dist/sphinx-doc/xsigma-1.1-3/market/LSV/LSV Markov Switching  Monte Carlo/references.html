“Pricing with a Smile”
Risk Magazine, Vol. 7, No. 1, pp. 18-20
Foundational work on local volatility models and the Dupire equation.

Review of Financial Studies, Vol. 6, No. 2, pp. 327-343
Seminal paper on stochastic volatility modeling.

[4] Lipton, A. (2002)
“The Vol Smile Problem”
Risk Magazine, Vol. 15, No. 2, pp. 61-65
Discussion of volatility smile modeling challenges and solutions.

“Calibrating and Pricing with Embedded Local Volatility Models”
Risk Magazine, Vol. 20, No. 9, pp. 138-143
Methodology for calibrating LSV models to market data.

[6] Guyon, J. and Henry-Labordère, P. (2011)
“The Smile Calibration Problem Solved”
Risk Magazine, Vol. 24, No. 1, pp. 90-96
Advanced techniques for LSV model calibration.

[7] Van der Stoep, A. W., Grzelak, L. A., and Oosterlee, C. W. (2014)
“The Heston Stochastic-Local Volatility Model: Efficient Monte Carlo Simulation”
International Journal of Theoretical and Applied Finance, Vol. 17, No. 7
Monte Carlo implementation techniques for LSV models.

Markov Switching Models#

[8] Hamilton, J. D. (1989)
“A New Approach to the Economic Analysis of Nonstationary Time Series and the Business Cycle”
Econometrica, Vol. 57, No. 2, pp. 357-384
Foundational work on Markov switching models in economics.

[9] Elliott, R. J., Aggoun, L., and Moore, J. B. (1995)
“Hidden Markov Models: Estimation and Control”
Springer-Verlag, New York
Comprehensive treatment of hidden Markov models and applications.

[10] Buffington, J. and Elliott, R. J. (2002)
“American Options with Regime Switching”
International Journal of Theoretical and Applied Finance, Vol. 5, No. 5, pp. 497-514
Application of Markov switching to option pricing.

Monte Carlo Methods#

[11] Glasserman, P. (2003)
“Monte Carlo Methods in Financial Engineering”
Springer-Verlag, New York
Comprehensive reference on Monte Carlo methods for finance.

[12] Longstaff, F. A. and Schwartz, E. S. (2001)
“Valuing American Options by Simulation: A Simple Least-Squares Approach”
Review of Financial Studies, Vol. 14, No. 1, pp. 113-147
Least-squares Monte Carlo method for American options.

[13] Broadie, M. and Glasserman, P. (1996)
“Estimating Security Price Derivatives Using Simulation”
Management Science, Vol. 42, No. 2, pp. 269-285
Techniques for computing Greeks via Monte Carlo simulation.

[14] Padla Framework Documentation
Internal Technical Documentation
Comprehensive guide to the Padla scripting language for exotic derivatives pricing, including life cycling and cash reporting functionalities.

[15] Cash Flow Reporting in Padla

Detailed documentation on cash flow generation and reporting capabilities within the Padla framework.

[16] Padla Life Cycling Procedures
Internal Operations Manual
Step-by-step procedures for trade life cycling, historical fixings, and exercise decision handling in Padla.

Risk Management and Validation#

[17] Cont, R. (2006)
“Model Uncertainty and its Impact on the Pricing of Derivative Instruments”
Mathematical Finance, Vol. 16, No. 3, pp. 519-547
Discussion of model risk and validation techniques.

[18] Rebonato, R. (2004)
“Volatility and Correlation: The Perfect Hedger and the Fox”
John Wiley & Sons, Chichester
Comprehensive treatment of volatility modeling and hedging.

[19] Derman, E. (1996)
“Model Risk”
Risk Magazine, Vol. 9, No. 5, pp. 139-145
Foundational discussion of model risk in derivatives pricing.

Numerical Methods#

[20] Press, W. H., Teukolsky, S. A., Vetterling, W. T., and Flannery, B. P. (2007)
“Numerical Recipes: The Art of Scientific Computing”
Cambridge University Press, 3rd Edition
Reference for numerical algorithms including matrix exponentials and optimization.

[21] Higham, N. J. (2005)
“The Scaling and Squaring Method for the Matrix Exponential Revisited”
SIAM Journal on Matrix Analysis and Applications, Vol. 26, No. 4, pp. 1179-1193
Advanced techniques for computing matrix exponentials.

Calibration and Optimization#

[22] Nocedal, J. and Wright, S. J. (2006)
“Numerical Optimization”
Springer-Verlag, New York, 2nd Edition
Comprehensive reference on optimization algorithms for model calibration.

[23] Levenberg, K. (1944)
“A Method for the Solution of Certain Non-Linear Problems in Least Squares”
Quarterly Journal of Applied Mathematics, Vol. 2, No. 2, pp. 164-168
Original Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm for nonlinear least squares.

Market Data and Volatility Surfaces#

[24] Fengler, M. R. (2005)
“Semiparametric Modeling of Implied Volatility”
Springer-Verlag, Berlin
Advanced techniques for volatility surface modeling and interpolation.

[25] Gatheral, J. (2006)
“The Volatility Surface: A Practitioner’s Guide”
John Wiley & Sons, Hoboken

“Monte Carlo Methods in Finance”
John Wiley & Sons, Chichester
Practical implementation of Monte Carlo methods with focus on performance optimization.

[27] Kloeden, P. E. and Platen, E. (1992)
“Numerical Solution of Stochastic Differential Equations”
